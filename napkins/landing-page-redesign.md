# Landing Page Redesign

## What this is

A full redesign of the Napkin landing page. The current page looks cheap — handwritten fonts, sketchy borders, and indie styling don't communicate the quality or purpose of what Napkin actually does. The copy doesn't sell the idea. This sub-napkin covers the visual and copy overhaul.

## Why

The current landing page undermines the product. Napkin has evolved from "turn ideas into docs" to "turn ideas into launchpads that orient agents toward the best possible version of your vision." The landing page still shows the old framing. First impressions matter — if the page looks cheap, people won't trust it with their ideas.

## Scope

**In:**
- Full visual redesign — move away from handwritten/sketch aesthetic toward premium, polished design
- New copy that communicates what Napkin actually does now (decision-surfacing, best-in-class default, heat-seeking missile)
- Keep the Napkin identity (📝, idea → doc, the name) but elevate the execution
- Dark/light mode (system detection)
- Three install paths (agent skill, chat prompt, CLI)
- Mobile-first responsive
- New NAPKIN.md content reflected in the copy

**Out:**
- Keeping the Caveat/Patrick Hand handwriting fonts as primary
- Keeping the sketch-border / asymmetric border-radius aesthetic
- Keeping the old copy ("Say napkin to your agent. Ramble your idea.")

## How it works

### Visual direction
Reference: Notion. Clean, confident, premium. Generous whitespace. Sans-serif typography (Inter as primary, not handwriting fonts). Product-forward — show what a NAPKIN.md looks like, show the convergence loop in action. Soft, warm color palette but not overly themed. The 📝 emoji can appear as a logo mark but the overall aesthetic should feel like a modern dev tool, not an art project.

Key visual principles:
- Clean sans-serif (Inter) for all body and headings. No handwriting fonts as primary.
- Generous spacing, consistent vertical rhythm
- Soft shadows, subtle depth
- Product screenshots/mockups as the hero (show a NAPKIN.md example)
- Minimal color — one accent, neutral backgrounds, dark/light
- No sketch borders, no asymmetric border-radius, no hand-drawn elements
- Premium feel: think Notion, Linear, Vercel — not Pinterest craft

### Copy direction
The copy needs to communicate the updated Napkin vision:
- Not just "turn ideas into docs" — "turn ideas into launchpads that orient your agent toward the best possible version of your vision"
- The heat-seeking missile concept: the napkin lights the rocket, your agent is the guidance system
- Best-in-class default: Napkin assumes you want quality, not just functional
- Decision-surfacing: Napkin identifies what decisions exist so your agent doesn't miss them
- Three-part outcome: everything you wanted + nothing you didn't + surprises you love
- The flow: describe your idea → Napkin asks questions → you get a doc → hand it to your agent → come back to something better than you imagined

### Hero section
- Headline that communicates the core promise (not "say napkin to your agent")
- Subheadline that explains the outcome
- Visual: mockup of a NAPKIN.md or the convergence loop in action
- CTA: install the skill or copy the prompt

### Sections
1. Hero — the promise
2. How it works — the flow (describe → questions → doc → build)
3. What makes it different — decision-surfacing, best-in-class, goal-oriented
4. Example — show a real NAPKIN.md output
5. Install — three paths (agent, chat, CLI)
6. Footer
